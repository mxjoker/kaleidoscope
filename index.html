⚓ Ship's Log
The Kaleidoscope · Port of Fenhollow
Session 2 — The Wreck of the Salted Meridian

The crew investigated the wreck of the Salted Meridian and found Captain Vess alive in the debris. They pulled him out and paid 3gp to get him care at the local inn.

Wayne used Kimchi's IOU to get the harbormaster talking — Vess's box had been sold for 50gp. The party took the gold back. Wayne's carnival manifest turned out to contain a sigil linked to a cult of elemental worshippers.

They tracked a woman named Maren Thoss to the Carrow Arms. After a failed con and Myrtle getting caught picking Thoss's pocket, she fled. The guards investigated but the party talked their way out. Thoss was on the run. Myrtle is wanted by the Port Carrow guards.

The crew chased Thoss to the Ironside Inn — broke in the back, waterboarded the front desk, kicked in the door — and found her in the middle of something they interrupted before they could see it through. They killed Thoss. Wayne used Rope Trick to shunt the chest into a pocket dimension before anything could get out. Whatever was in there is still in there. Nobody has discussed what that means.

Total haul: 180gp split seven ways. The captain's personal box is still missing — known to contain a magic sextant. Next session: Ratstew Gap.

Note: Most of the crew came down with something in the days after. Probably the water. Probably nothing. Everyone's running at half health heading into the next job.

Delivered to the Kaleidoscope — addressed to the crew
To whoever captains this vessel, and those who sail with them.

My name is Brother Mazlo Merryshadow. I write on behalf of myself and Sister Rosy Rumblegarden, servants of Fharlanghn, god of roads and wanderers. We are in desperate need of an escort.

We set out three weeks ago with a caravan of twelve to cross the Grandspire Mountains to Ratstew Gap, where we are to consecrate a shrine — a pilgrimage we have prepared for two years. The caravan did not make it. We will not describe what happened in the passes. We are alive. Most were not.

We are stranded at the base of the mountains. We have our faith, a dog named Buttercup, and approximately forty gold pieces between us. We are not fighters. We would not ask this of strangers, but Fharlanghn places the right people on the right roads. We believe you may be those people.

The shrine must be built. We have come too far.

— Br. Mazlo Merryshadow & Sr. Rosy Rumblegarden, Pilgrims of Fharlanghn

Wayne's spellbook was heavier when he came back from backstage. Mass increase: approx 340g. Smells of incense. Filed under: Interesting (Category: VERY).
Entry 9 — Looking Ahead ▼
Have been researching the Salted Meridian. Four theories. Three involve Captain Vess being alive. One involves something worse. Showed Dred'ban theory two. He did a very long silence — approximately 8.4 seconds. This is the longest recorded. Filing as: Confirmation (Probable). Also: three shipping manifests. The navigational instrument is not on the recovery list. Neither is the captain. Filed under: This Is A Problem.
Entry 13 — Port Carrow to Fenhollow ▼
Captain Vess is alive. This is consistent with theories one, two, and three. Theory four remains unconfirmed. Have not discarded it. Chest is still in Wayne's pocket dimension. No one has opened it. This is either very disciplined or very afraid. Filed under: Ambiguous. Myrtle's wanted status in Port Carrow is a variable I have been tracking. Her evasion coefficient is high. Dred'ban said nothing when I told him. He looked at the horizon for eleven seconds. New record. Filed under: Dred'ban: A Study in Silence, Entry 7 (Subentry: Horizon Incidents). A letter arrived for the crew before we left. Two halflings. Mountains. Vow. Dog named Buttercup. Mass estimate: small but determined. Dred'ban picked up the letter and put it back down without reading it. He's coming anyway. Filed under: Confirmed (Category: Crew).
